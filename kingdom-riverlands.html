About The Riverlands

The Riverlands is a fertile region in central Westeros, wealthy and prosperous in times of peace. It is ruled by House Tully from their seat at Riverrun, a castle built at the confluence of three rivers. The Riverlands is known for its agricultural abundance and strategic position. House Tully values family bonds and loyalty above all else. However, as a central location, the Riverlands frequently became a battleground during the wars.

Geography & Culture

The Riverlands are defined by numerous rivers that provide both fertility and transportation. The region is less heavily forested than others and more open, making it easier to traverse but harder to defend. Riverrun is a formidable fortress surrounded by water. The people of the Riverlands are traders and farmers, and they value the old ways of chivalry and honor. The region follows the Faith of the Seven.

Notable Events

The Riverlands suffered greatly during the War of the Five Kings, serving as a major battlefield. Robb Stark's armies marched through the region, and numerous battles were fought on Tully lands. The Red Wedding, one of the most shocking events in the series, took place in the Riverlands. The region was eventually conquered and fell under various rulers before achieving stability.
