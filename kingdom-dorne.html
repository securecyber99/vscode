About Dorne

Dorne is the most unique and distinctive of the Six Kingdoms, known for its desert landscape and culturally different population. It is ruled by House Martell from their fortress at Sunspear. The Dornish people are passionate, tempestuous, and known for their sensuality and independence. Dorne was the last kingdom to join the Six Kingdoms and has maintained a strong sense of individuality and pride. The house words reflect Dorne's unconquerable spirit—"Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken."

Geography & Culture

Dorne is dominated by the Red Mountains and vast deserts, with a hot climate vastly different from northern regions. The landscape makes invasion difficult, and the Dornish are skilled at desert warfare. Sunspear is built on golden sands and reflects the unique architecture of the region. The Dornish follow a modified version of the Faith of the Seven, blended with older traditions. The culture is more liberal and open than other kingdoms, with different attitudes toward inheritance and gender roles.

Notable Events

Dorne remained independent for longer than any other kingdom, only joining through marriage rather than conquest. The region escaped the worst ravages of the War of the Five Kings due to its distance and strategic positioning. Dorne maintained important alliances with other houses through strategic marriages. Oberyn's participation in the trial by combat against The Mountain was a pivotal moment. Dorne ultimately played a crucial role in the final outcomes of the series through their political maneuvering and alliances.
