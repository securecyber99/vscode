About The North

The North is the largest kingdom in Westeros by area, comprising roughly one-third of the continent. It is a cold, harsh land known for its winters that can last for years. The North is ruled by House Stark from their seat at Winterfell. The region is known for its strong sense of honor, loyalty, and the practice of keeping one's word. The Starks are renowned for their Northern discipline and their connection to the old gods of the forest.

Geography & Culture

The North encompasses vast forests, mountains, and the Wall in the far north, which separates the Six Kingdoms from the lands beyond. The climate is perpetually cold, and the inhabitants are hardy and resilient. The North values honor, duty, and the old ways. The people of the North practice the religion of the Old Gods rather than the Faith of the Seven.

Notable Events

The North saw significant change during the series. Winterfell was captured by Theon Greyjoy but reclaimed by the Starks. The North was the base for Robb Stark's War of the Five Kings. Later, it fell under the control of the Boltons before Jon Snow retook it. The North remained strong and proud throughout the conflicts, ultimately achieving independence or self-governance by the end of the series.
